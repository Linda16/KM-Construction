KM Construction expands its healthcare client base to six London-based NHS Trusts

08/01/2014

Having completed a project to refurbish nine rooms at The Heart Hospital, which is part of University College London Hospital (UCLH), KM Construction, the London-based specialist construction company, has won two further projects in the healthcare sector.

Repeat NHS Trust business for KM Construction

02/01/2014

The London-based specialist construction company, KM Construction, has won a further contract from the Imperial College Healthcare NHS Trust. This time, the work is at St Mary’s Hospital and involves a partial refurbishment of an existing cardiac catheter ward. The result of KM Construction’s work will be a new Endoscopy Scope Decontamination Unit at the hospital. Paul Driver, KM Construction’s Business Development Director, explained: “Having stripped out the ward, our team will install new doorways, strengthen the existing floor and carry out modifications and enhancements to the mechanical and electrical installations – in particular, installing new equipment for cleaning, drying and packaging scopes within a clinically clean environment.” The project, which is scheduled to be completed in March next year, is the second contract which the company has won from the Imperial College Healthcare NHS Trust.

Upper Berkeley Street project gets underway

01/12/2014

KM Construction continue its good working relationship with Portman Estates having been appointed to carry out the extensive refurbishment and extension of a 7,500 square feet office building in London’s West End. The Scheme was initially proposed in 2013, although it took some time for the project to obtain planning permission. The whole project - to refurbish and extend 12A Upper Berkeley Street – is under the direction of Campbell Architects and is scheduled to be completed in December 2015. As principal Contractor for the scheme our work involves alterations and fit out to, the basement, ground, first, second, third and fourth floors of the property to provide ‘CAT A’ office accommodation. This includes demolishing some existing parts of the building; carrying out structural works including the replacement of suspended slabs at ground, second, third and fourth floor levels. We will also be raising the building’s roof level and adding some steelwork as part of some general re-roofing works.
